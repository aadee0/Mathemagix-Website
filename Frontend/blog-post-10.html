How to Deeply Analyze Mock Test Results

Published on Feb 02, 2025 • By Dr. Rajesh Kumar

Taking a 3-hour mock test is useless if you don't spend at least 2 hours analyzing it afterward. Your score tells you where you are, but your analysis tells you how to move forward.

The 3-Category Analysis Method

After checking your answers, categorize every single question into one of three buckets:

  • Category A (Silly Mistakes): You knew the concept but misread the question, messed up a basic calculation, or marked the wrong bubble. Fix: Better focus and double-checking calculations.
  • Category B (Concept Gap): You applied a formula, but it was the wrong one, or you only knew half the theory. Fix: Re-read the chapter notes immediately.
  • Category C (Clueless): You had no idea how to even start. Fix: Discuss with a teacher; you need to relearn this specific topic from scratch.

Track Your "Time Leaks"

Did a single math integration question take you 12 minutes? That is a time leak. Even if you got it right, spending 12 minutes on one question meant you sacrificed three easier questions at the end of the paper. Identify which topics cause you to bleed time and practice them with a stopwatch.
