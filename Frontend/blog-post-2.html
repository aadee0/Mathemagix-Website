10 Mistakes Students Make in Board Exams

Published on May 10, 2025 • By Ms. Kavita Joshi

Many bright students who excel in JEE mock tests end up losing marks in their Class 12 CBSE Board Exams. Why? Because competitive exams test your final answer, while board exams test your process.

1. Skipping the "Given" and "To Prove" Steps

Examiners look for a structured approach. Always start by explicitly writing down the data provided in the question and what you are trying to find or prove. This alone carries step marks.

2. Poor Diagram Representation

In subjects like Physics or Mathematics (especially 3D Geometry and Calculus applications), drawing a neat, labeled diagram is non-negotiable. Use a pencil, not a pen, so you can make corrections.

3. Ignoring Units

Writing an answer as "45" instead of "45 cm²" is a guaranteed way to lose half a mark. Always double-check your final units, especially in surface area, volume, and physics derivations.

4. Overwriting and Messy Calculations

If you make a mistake, draw a single neat line through it. Scribbling makes your paper look chaotic and frustrates the examiner. Do rough work in a dedicated margin on the right side of the page, not at the bottom.
