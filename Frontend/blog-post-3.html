Spaced Repetition in Math Learning

Published on April 28, 2025 • By Prof. Anita Sharma

Have you ever mastered a complex integration technique, only to completely forget how to apply it a month later during a mock test? You are experiencing the "Forgetting Curve."

What is Spaced Repetition?

Spaced repetition is a learning technique that incorporates increasing intervals of time between subsequent reviews of previously learned material. It exploits the psychological spacing effect to maximize long-term retention.

How to Apply it to Mathematics

  • Day 1: Learn the concept (e.g., Integration by Parts) and solve 20 varied problems.
  • Day 3: Do not re-read the theory. Jump straight into solving 5 medium-level problems.
  • Day 10: Attempt 3 high-difficulty (JEE Advanced level) problems on the topic.
  • Day 30: Include this topic in a mixed-bag mock test.

The "Flashcard" Myth for Math

While flashcards are great for chemistry formulas, mathematics requires you to remember processes, not just facts. Instead of flashcards, keep a "Mistake Journal." Reviewing the specific logical missteps you made previously is the most powerful form of spaced repetition for a math student.
